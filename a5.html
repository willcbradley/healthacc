All In One Direction

Ever since starting medical school, it's felt like I'm being pulled in two directions at once.

One one side, the predictable (but suffocating) structure or medical tradition. On the other, the inspiring (but disorienting) potential of the frontier.

For years, I've spent all my spare time and attention seraching for a non-contradictory way to pursue these paths in unison. Some way of defining the problem neatly, so that all of my efforts are pulling me in a single direction.

But we've just established that this is impossible.

There are no rules any more. Everything is moving too fast to be predictable. The value of regulatory protection has never been higher.

But this protection comes at a cost, and it's our job to quantify this cost, and pay it (or not) accordingly.

Try as we might, we can never reduce this problem to a one-dimensional equation.

Instead, it comes down to developing the skill of managing the inherent tension.

Funnily enough, developing that skill, in and of itself, is the single direction we should've been heading in all along.
