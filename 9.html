What is technology?

Ideas don't implement themselves.

In theory, new scientific ideas often have practical applications. In practice, things are more complex.

Applying these concepts to reality requires repeated designing, testing, and iteration. We call the output of this process technology.

Across history, technological progress has followed a smooth exponential curve (although it's often bumpy in the short term).

To hang on as things speed up, it helps to understand the mechanics of this process.
