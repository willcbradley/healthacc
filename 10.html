What makes an engineer?

Nature is harsh.

Unambiguous feedback leaves nowhere to hide. And with nowhere to hide, we're forced to adapt.

Fundamentally, it seems like we learn to create new technology (defined here as "engineering") by exposing ourselves to as much of this feedback as possible.

The trick, then, is to aim for maximal contact with reality, and minimal fluff. Since most fields of engineering have some fixed amount of arbitrary conventions to learn, it seems smart to start by going deeper in one particular field.

Then, once the core skills and heuristics are understood, other fields become more accessible, and our scope of projects can expand.

How far they expand, only time will tell.
