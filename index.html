health/acc
thesis | projects
The world is speeding up.

Technology creates leverage, which creates uncertainty, which threatens our medical institutions. health/acc is an attempt to define this problem (so we can solve it while we have the chance).

Consider two opposing forces: tradition and progress.

If healthcare moves too slowly, it will devolve into negative-sum rent-seeking. But if we let new inventions run wild, the mistakes could be unacceptable.

In the most likely case, we end up with doctors trying to justify useless rules, and technologists trying to discard vital ones.

How to prepare for this?

By combining the best of both worlds.

The faster the frontier moves, the greater the distance between labs and wards. To prevent collapse, we need people who can speak (and teach) both languages.

In practice, producing these people is difficult.

Current incentives push us toward single, narrow status games. Only by truly learning both skill sets, as individuals, can we carve a path for future generations.

This is no small task. It'll take decades to pan out (if at all).

But health is priceless. Our bodies stubbornly work to keep us alive against all odds. Without fighting entropy, we have nothing.

If you see what I see, let me know.

- Will Bradley
