What Could Medicine Become?

Tech is improving. Fast.

If history continues, we're going to get rapidly better at fighting biological stressors.

But as the science of medicine accelerates, the art will lag behind.

Incumbents will cling to traditions, as they've always done. But in some cases, their caution will be warranted.

The stakes have never been higher. If we get this wrong, billions will die. That's the whole point of the health/acc thesis.

But assume we're successful. Assume we survive. What then?

What are we working towards? What can we look forward to? What might we imagine, when we think of what's to come?

Imagine a world where cancer is nothing more than a nuisance. A world where your surgeon is built in a factory, and trained in a data center as it orbits Earth.

Imagine a future where the average 80-year-old has never known of dementia. Where they spend more time chasing their grandchildren, and less time chasing up medical referrals.

Imagine mapping the microbiome. Or giving genome-specific antibiotics. Or doubling global life expectancy.

No doubt these are naive predictions. No doubt there will be unforseen challenges. But they're solvable.

The only way out is through. We will build an incredible future, or die trying.
