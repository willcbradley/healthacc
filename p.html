What's The Price Of Becoming A Doctor?

Hard work is hard.

Many of today's jobs involve on-call, or long hours, or night shift. Although these costs are real, they'll change with time and circumstances.

But now that we've defined medicine, and defined the inputs required to become a doctor, we should be able to map out the inherent (i.e. unchanging) downsides that come with the territory. Let's see what we can come up with.

Healthcare requires trust.

Insofar as doctors aren't obsolete, it's because patients rely on them (including for important, difficult decisions).

But trust scales poorly.

Beyond a certain population size, social ties become weak enough that shame can't be used to enforce the rules. So, in place of trust, we create laws and regulations.

In one sense, this is crucial. Laws provide structure, and help us move forward. But on the other hand, these rules are disconnected from reality — they're backed by power, rather than nature or human nature.

The further a game is disconnected from reality, the more arbitrary the rules can become. Inevitably, things devolve into a status contest — impractical at best, corrupt and demoralising at worst.

And so, the inevitable price of becoming a doctor is playing this game. Enduring the lack of meritocracy. Putting up with arbitrary requirements. All while incurring massive opportunity cost in the process.

Luckily, this price is paid voluntarily.

If the transaction no longer makes sense, we can opt out. Provided we're building the right foundations, our fundamental skills can be transferred anywhere else.

Technology is moving too quickly for us to be blindly chasing status, but culture is moving too slowly for us to reject it entirely.

What to do, then?

Play the game, with eyes wide open.

Learn to survive outside the status contest, and then voluntarily and conditionally adopt the rules for as long as makes sense.

Either the price is worth paying, or it isn't. Either we go full steam ahead, or change direction entirely. The worst decision of all is to do anything in between.

Maybe we fail. Maybe technology shreds civilisation apart at the seams.

I say we go down swinging.
