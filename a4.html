Here Are The Frontiers

Frontiers are fragile.

All it takes is a few arbitrary rules for everything to come crashing down.

Why is this important?

Because it makes it almost impossible for fast-paced frontiers to exist in clinical practice.

Even the most urgent developments are subject to scrutiny and regulation — this sluggishness, justified or not, kills iteration speed (and therefore prevents fast progress).

The status game does not exist on accident. It has grown as a function of the sheltered environment, and will remain for as long as there are humans in healthcare.

The true frontiers, by necessity, are elsewhere. Out of reach of regulators, and out of sight of the broader market.

As the world speeds up, those frontiers will move farther and farther from the rest of society.

How to solve this? Is it solvable?
