What moves science forward?

The basic algorithm is simple.

We make a theory, test the theory, keep what works, and discard the rest.

But in practice, things are more complex.

Real-world 'science' is muddled by resource constraints, short-term sentiment, and status games.

How to navigate this?

By securing upside, and removing downside.

Overcoming resource constraints, without losing sight of the overall trajectory. Listening to sentiment, without losing sight of the long-term trajectory. Surviving the status game, without depending on it.

We walk a fine line. On one side, impracticality, and on the other, short-sightedness.

Unless we strike a balance, there will be no future.
